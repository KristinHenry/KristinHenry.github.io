Do people like to get letters? How long does it take for them to arrive?

In the early Covid years, I was curious about these questions, and started mailing stickers through the US Postal system. I'm still mailing them.

Folks sign up, I mail a sticker to them, and then they tell me when it arrives.

It's a perfectly imperfect data collection method, and thousands of people have joined the fun of my #StickersAndStamps project.

Sitting at my desk, I found myself staring at a pile of stickers. I was going to give them away at events.

But everything was canceled...because of Covid. This was early 2020.

What was I going to do with all these stickers?

I started mailing them to friends, and asked them to tell me when they arrived. I was curious to see how long it would take for my letters to get to my friends.

Within a few weeks, I had a nice little data set. It surprised me how quickly some letters arrived, and how much longer it took for others. Would more data show more patterns?

I posted the project online, and within a few months, I’d mailed out over 300 handwritten notes and stickers.

How long did it take for these first letters to reach their destinations?

Most of my letters were delivered, in the US, within 2–4 days. But many of them took a lot longer.

The distance an envelope traveled did not correspond with how long it took to be delivered. If it did, the lines in this chart would all be mostly straight up and down.

International Mail

International mail, as expected, takes longer than most domestic mail.

And it takes a very long time for it to get to Australia.

Domestic USPS Mail

Delivery times of domestic mail varies quite a bit.

These dots represent individual letters I have mailed. The color represents the number of days it took to arrive. The more orange, the more days.

See those orange dots, up in Canada? Crossing the border and into the Canadian postal service, can be slow.

USPS Sorting Centers

The US Postal Service utilizes many regional sorting centers. Your mail goes, in and out, through the center that serves your zip code.

Some areas have several sorting centers nearby, and others are served by sorting centers in another state!

Here, the sorting centers my letters have passed through are drawn as open circles. The lines connect the town where the letter was delivered with the regional sorting center that letter passed through.

Zooming in

By the end of 2020, my data set was still small. But even with 300 letters (data points), we can already see that some sorting centers deliver promptly. Others do not.

If I had more data, I could possibly dismiss some of the slow deliveries from centers that are usually quick. Or maybe it differs by zipcode? I may look at that, if I get more participants.

Western States

Being less densely populated, the western states have fewer sorting centers than Eastern states.

Note that not all sorting centers are shown on this map. Only the sorting centers that my letters have passed through are shown.

Denver, CO

Denver has some prompt deliveries and some slow ones, and these are all passing through the same sorting center.

Fast forward...to 2022

As of July 2022, I've mailed out over 3,000 envelopes.

That's a lot of dots on our map!

While I've sent out over 3,000 envelopes, not everyone told me when their's arrived.

A surge in sugnups, in mid-2021 left me exhausted and burnt-out. I was looking forward to growing my little data set, but most of the folks who signed up just ingnored my request to report arrival time.

But in later surge events, folks were a lot better about helping me with the data part of the project.

The locations I have mailed letters to, but do not have arrival times for, are marked as white dots on this map.
